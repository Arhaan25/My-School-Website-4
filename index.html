What's New

  • Visit To Old Age Home

    On 30th May 2022, the students of Grade XII Psychology Department of St. Xavier’s High School went on a field trip to The Earth Saviour’s Foundation. A total of thirteen students went on this visit accompanied by Ms. Honey Sharma (Psychology Teacher) and Ms. Ritika George (Career Counsellor). The ration was loaded for donation at the foundation and the bus immediately left the school premises. On reaching the destination the team was greeted by the members of the foundation. The students and teachers were briefed by Mr. Jas Kalra, the current President of The Earth Saviour’s Foundation. He shared valuable morals and inspiring experiences. The teachers and students then proceeded to interact with the people inhabited in the foundation. Initially the Psychology Department presented Mr. Kalra with a sapling and a card made by the students. Later, the members of the foundation felicitated the team with trophies, magazines and certificates to acknowledge the voluntary visit. Post the educational visit the students returned to the school premises.

  • Xavierians’ plea for healing the world and making it a better place to live!

    Xavierians’ plea for healing the world and making it a better place to live! ‘The real and lasting victories are those of peace and not of war.’ History has witnessed a multitude of wars which have taken place during different timelines and eras between different countries. The consequences of war are detrimental to a country and the society at large. Russia-Ukraine War testifies this. War is no solution rather peace is the pathway to development and prosperity for a society. In the absence of peace and harmony, achieving political strength, cultural growth and economic stability will not be possible. Xaverians believe that sustaining tranquility is not an individual responsibility but a shared goal of the whole society. Keeping this vision in mind, our compassionate Xaverians of Grade VII and VIII presented a ‘Rally - Peace, Unity and Harmony’ and raised slogans urging Russia and Ukraine to end the war. Thought-provoking speech by young Xaverians emphasised the significance of peace and solicited the audience to spread the message of love. There was also an inspiring dance and musical performance followed by citing slogans for curbing violence in any form. Parents expressed their appreciation for the great initiative the school has taken to stand up and help make a difference.
